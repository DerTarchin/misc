Private Law Firms vs
Public Defense Offices

One major factor that helps establish the funding and caseload crisis of public offices is comparing them with private law firms. In 2010, 12% of county-funded public defense offices recieved over 5000 cases, and the average number of attorneys in public offices across the US is a measly 15. A public defender can work any number of years in these stressful office conditions, but their salaries will never exceed the maximum authorized value of $68k a year. In private firms, the longer you work, the better you get paid. Partners that have been with a firm for over 15-20 years can have salaries over a million dollars.

As is the nature with private institutions, it is not common to find publically released statistics regarding employee salaries, annual expidentures, number of employees, etc. However, through some deduction using average and ideal statistics, these statistics have been compiled to compare private law firms with state or county funded public defense offices.

The compass chart on the left represents four datasets. the north-most datapoints represent the average salary for full-time attorneys. East represents the estimated average revenue for major private firms compared with the average expidentures for public offices. South compares how long attorneys stay in the office and west compares the average number of cases per office or firm.

Data collected through sources including The Bureau of Justice Statistics, work.chron.com, ABA Journal, and Inc magazine.
